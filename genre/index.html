The differences between various types of electronic dance music can be quite subtle and can confuse even the most avid of listeners. Having a general idea of what each subgenre sounds like will help you use our filter to display music that appeals directly to your taste buds. Here is the easiest way to identify each type of Roast:

Big Room Progressive House
Characterized mainly by choruses with large-sounding chords that are often piano-based, Big Room tracks typically consist of a simple melody, which is played throughout the song in various ways. These songs are likely to be happy and uplifting causing even the largest of crowds to jump towards the sky.

Dance
Dance songs are usually pop songs that have been remixed with a steady, danceable beat. Unlike most other subgenres, these tracks tend to incorporate several different lyrical verses. They may even feature rapping. Although Dance tracks often borrow elements from other subgenres, they retain the mass appeal of pop songs by diluting these elements to some extent. These tracks are designed to get everyone dancing, not just EDM fans.

Drum & Bass
Songs within the Drum & Bass category are easily distinguished by their fast-paced, alternating rhythm of snare and kick drums in conjunction with fast hi-hats. These tracks bring percussion to the forefront of the soundstage and are known to cause fidgety hypnosis.

A nearly perfect balance of progressiveness and wobble. The vocals fit nicely as well. A must listen.

Dubstep
Oscillating bass, or wobble as it more commonly referred to as, is the staple of these songs. The percussion is somewhat similar to the type used in rock music. The rhythm sometimes sounds like Drum & Bass, but the bass is more prominently featured while the snare and kick drums are slightly recessed in comparison. The intensity of Dubstep can range from tame to wild. The lack of a steady beat distinguishes Dubstep from songs within the Electro House subgenre, which often share many similar sounds.

Dutch House
The tracks within this variant of house use pitch-bent melodies for dramatic effect. The result can be described as sounding like an ambulance's siren, or whining. More commonly referred to as bleeps, these sounds are quite distinct and they add an element of intensity to any environment.

Electro House
Distorted and edgy sounds dominate this subgenre. Depending on the level of complexity, a wide variety of samples can be used, which are often sporadically placed throughout a track. However, one constant within this subgenre is the bass, which usually has a unique buzzing sound to it. Listeners might also hear some wobble in certain songs, but the steady beat distinguishes songs within this subgenre from those within Dubstep.

Nu Disco
As the name of this subgenre implies, retro sounds are commonly used to convey a sense of nostalgia within Nu Disco tracks. Listeners will even encounter vocal samples taken from original disco songs, which are usually soul-influenced. Other traditional instruments, such as electric guitars and vintage keyboard synths, are often heard as well. The variety of sounds that are found within this subgenre is immense, but the modern twist on disco should be apparent along with the desire to boogie-down.

Progressive House
Most modern house songs contain some progressive elements, but true progressive house songs are distinguished by their melodies. Songs within this subgenre will routinely introduce a simple melody early in the track, and will then build upon this melody with a variety of complimentary harmonies. These tracks seem to morph, and the final drop is rarely the same as the first, unlike Big Room songs, which tend to use the same melody throughout the entire song. Some of the most beautifully composed tracks are found within this subgenre.

Tech House
Tech House songs customarily have a rolling bass line and small, quick taps on a hi-hat. The result can be quite funky and is typically bouncy in feel. Modern Tech House pieces also incorporate unusual vocal samples, which are often short, fractional slices of lyrics, and are used as additional percussive elements. These songs tend to be more laid-back than other house variants and have been warming-up crowds for decades.

Techno
Contrary to popular belief, Techno is not an all-encompassing term for electronic music. Techno is actually a subgenre of electronic dance music. Driving bass lines are commonplace in Techno tracks. Double, triple, and even quadruple bass kicks are used to create a deep vibe. Many modern techno songs border on the boundaries of Progressive and Tech House, but the relentless bass line is distinct enough to warrant separate categorization due to the uniquely dark and serious mood that results.

Trance
The tempo of various house tracks rarely exceeds 130 beats per minute (BPM). However, the tempo of Trance songs is almost exclusively faster than 130 BPM. Tracks falling within this subgenre often feature sweeping synths, which swing back and forth between the left and right channels. The resulting experience can be described as immersive. These songs also incorporate a lot of reverb, or echo, in order to expand the soundstage causing melodies to seemingly linger in the air.
